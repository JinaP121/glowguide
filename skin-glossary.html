Skin Health Guide Skin Glossary

Essential Skincare Ingredient

A. Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs)
  • AHAs help exfoliate dead skin cells and prevent aging.
  • They are plant- and animal-derived acids used in many skincare products.
  • People use AHAs to remove dead skin and treat skin concerns like acne and dullness.
V. Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
  • One of the most effective antioxidants in skincare.
  • Helps prevent excessive pigmentation and protects the skin from UV-induced aging.
  • Boosts collagen production, making the skin look firm and bright.
C. Ceramides
  • Ceramides are lipid molecules that help maintain a healthy skin barrier.
  • Skincare products containing ceramides replenish the skin barrier, preventing dryness and infections.
H. Hyaluronic Acid (HA)
  • A powerful humectant that can hold up to 1,000 times its weight in water, drawing and retaining moisture in the skin.
  • It enhances elasticity and gives the skin a plump, hydrated appearance.
R. Retinol (Vitamin A Derivative)
  • A form of Vitamin A that helps prevent skin aging.
  • Unlike other ingredients that work on the skin’s surface, retinol penetrates deeper layers to stimulate collagen and elastin production.
  • It hydrates the skin, fades dark spots, and treats acne and psoriasis.
  • Recommended Usage: Use at 0.04% concentration, apply at night before moisturizer.

Sun Protection

SPF (UVB Protection Index)
  • SPF measures protection against UVB rays (which cause sunburn). Higher SPF = stronger protection.
  • SPF 50 = theoretically 750 minutes of protection, but sweat, oil, water, and friction reduce effectiveness. Reapply every 2–3 hours.
  • Apply enough sunscreen (2mg/cm²) for full protection—most people use too little.
  • Higher SPF products contain more oil, so acne-prone skin should be careful.
PA (UVA Protection Index)
  • UVA rays penetrate deep into the skin and cause aging.
  • PA+ to PA++++ indicates protection level—more ‘+’ means stronger protection.
  • UVA passes through windows, so choose PA+++ or higher for daily use.
Chemical Sunscreen
  • Absorbs UV rays and converts them into heat energy.
  • No white cast and lightweight texture for easy application.
  • Absorbs into the skin, so may irritate sensitive skin.
  • Apply 30 minutes before sun exposure for full protection.
IRF (Infrared Radiation Factor)
  • Protects skin from infrared rays (heat from the sun) that cause skin temperature rise and aging.
  • Recommended for outdoor activities to prevent sun damage.
How to Choose the Right Sunscreen

(Based on TPO - Time, Place, Occasion)

  • For makeup base → Use physical sunscreen
  • For outdoor activities → Choose IRF protection + waterproof formula
  • For indoor use → SPF 30 with a light texture
Physical Sunscreen
  • Reflects UV rays instead of absorbing them → Instant protection from UVA & UVB.
  • Gentle on sensitive skin because it sits on the skin’s surface.
  • May leave a white cast and can feel thick.
